You asked an AI for a health plan. It gave you seven hours of sleep, thirty minutes of cardio three times a week, a Mediterranean diet, and a note to "reduce stress where possible." You read it, thought "that's reasonable," and did nothing with it — because it described a person with a completely different life than yours.

That's not a failure of effort. It's a failure of calibration. The AI built a plan for an average person who doesn't exist, rather than for a specific person who does. If your mornings are already spoken for, if your travel schedule blows up any fixed routine, if "reduce stress" is advice that requires its own plan to implement — then a wellness plan that ignores all of that isn't a wellness plan. It's a list of intentions with nowhere to land.

The Health & Wellness Plan skill works differently. Before it writes a single recommendation, it asks what your life actually looks like — your schedule, your current habits, your primary goal, your real constraints. Then it builds something that survives contact with your actual week.

Why Generic Plans Don't Stick

The standard AI wellness output has a recognisable shape: sleep consistency, hydration targets, some form of movement, a nudge toward whole foods, a stress-management technique. Each piece of advice is correct in isolation. Together, they describe a lifestyle that requires rebuilding your entire day from scratch.

The problem isn't the advice — it's the assumption baked into it. Generic wellness output assumes you're starting from zero with a blank calendar, unlimited willpower, and no competing obligations. Most people reading a wellness plan are not starting from zero. They're trying to fit something new into a life that is already full.

💡
The core problem

A wellness plan that doesn't account for your real schedule, real sleep patterns, and real stress load isn't a plan — it's aspirational fiction. The gap between the plan and your life is where it fails.

What's missing is the step that comes before writing: understanding how the person's life is actually structured. A founder with back-to-back calls from 8am has a different movement window than a freelancer with a flexible morning. A parent of a toddler has a different sleep reality than someone without dependents. A plan that doesn't register those differences will recommend the same things to both of them — and work for neither.

What Context Calibration Changes About the Output

The Health & Wellness Plan skill starts with three questions before it generates anything. Those questions aren't decorative — they're how the skill earns the right to give advice. They cover your primary goal (weight, energy, sleep, stress, fitness, or a combination), your current schedule and where the real constraints sit, and your existing habits — what's already working, even partially.

That intake shapes everything downstream. A person whose primary bottleneck is sleep quality gets a plan structured around sleep hygiene first, with movement and nutrition slotted into windows that don't undermine it. A person whose primary goal is energy during a demanding work stretch gets a plan built around recovery and sustainable output, not peak performance metrics that require rest days they don't have.

The skill doesn't produce a better generic plan. It produces a different kind of plan entirely — one built around specific constraints, not despite them.

The research component matters here too. The skill pulls current thinking on habit formation, recovery science, and evidence-based wellness protocols — not cached generalities, but what's actually holding up as useful for people trying to make changes while managing real workloads. That context shapes the structure, the sequencing, and the framing of every recommendation in the output.

Energy & Crash Reduction Plan — Week 1 Focus

Root cause (based on your profile): Your 6-hour sleep window combined with back-to-back calls until 3pm means your cortisol rhythm has no natural recovery trough. The crash at 2:30–3pm is predictable — it's not willpower, it's physiology.
