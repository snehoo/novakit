You have three minutes to make thirty people feel something real. Maybe it's at your best friend's wedding, your colleague's last day, your sister's birthday dinner. You know what you want to say. The problem is that every draft you write sounds like something you've heard before — and you know it, which is why you keep deleting it.

You open Claude and ask it to write a wedding toast. What you get back is a speech with the right shape: opening warmth, a funny anecdote placeholder, heartfelt close. But it doesn't sound like you knew the couple. It doesn't reference the moment she called you from the airport at 11pm to tell you he was the one. It doesn't know that he's terrified of public speaking and will be watching from the front row hoping you don't embarrass him. It doesn't know any of that — because you didn't tell it, and it didn't ask.

That gap is exactly what the Event Speech Writer skill closes.

Why Generic Structure Isn't the Problem

The structure of a good speech isn't a secret. Open strong, anchor it in a specific story, build toward something emotional, close with a forward-looking wish. Anyone can find this formula in five minutes. The problem isn't that people don't know the shape — it's that they can't find the material to fill it with.

When you ask a general-purpose AI to write a speech, it produces the formula. What it can't produce is the substance that makes the formula disappear. An audience doesn't hear "structure" when a great speech is happening — they hear a person, talking about a specific person, in a way that only they could.

💡
The core problem

A speech written without knowing the relationship, the occasion, and the room is just a template with names filled in — and audiences can tell the difference in the first thirty seconds.

This is what makes event speeches uniquely hard for AI to get right. The failure isn't stylistic — it's informational. The right details exist in your head, but getting them out in the right order and in the right proportions is where most people get stuck. They either write something too safe and forgettable, or they try to include everything and end up with a speech that rambles for seven minutes while people stare at their plates.

What Calibrated Questions Actually Unlock

Before the Event Speech Writer skill writes a single line, it interviews you. Not with a long form to fill out — three questions, designed to surface the raw material that only you have.

The questions change depending on the occasion. A retirement speech needs different material than a best man speech. A toast at a promotion dinner requires a different emotional register than a eulogy. The skill knows this — it's built for each occasion type, so the questions it asks are calibrated to extract exactly what that specific speech needs.

The skill earns the right to write by understanding the relationship first — not by assuming every speech needs the same three questions.

For a wedding toast, it might ask you to describe the moment you knew the couple was right for each other — not a general impression, but a specific scene. For a retirement speech, it might ask what the person being honoured would say if asked what they were most proud of. These aren't softballs. They're the questions that pull out the material that turns a forgettable speech into one people reference at future gatherings.

How the Event Speech Writer Skill Works

The skill runs entirely inside your Claude account — no new platform, no login, no account to create. You paste it in, and it takes over from there.

1
Occasion and role calibration
The skill identifies what kind of speech you're writing and your relationship to the person being honoured. A colleague writing a retirement speech sounds different from a daughter writing one for her father. The skill adjusts the tone, structure, and material prompts accordingly.
2
The three-question interview
You're asked three targeted questions designed to surface specific, usable material: a memory, a quality, a moment. The skill doesn't ask you to "describe them in a few words" — it asks for the scene that proves the quality, because scenes are what speeches are made of.
3
Tone and length targeting
You specify the room: formal dinner, relaxed garden wedding, office farewell. You specify the length you need — two minutes or five. The skill builds to those constraints, not beyond them, because a speech that runs over always lands worse than one that ends on time.
4
Draft generation with quality gate
The first draft goes through an automatic quality check before it reaches you. Clichéd openers, weak transitions, vague sentiment — flagged and rewritten. What you receive is a speech built on your specific material, not a template with your name swapped in.
5
Delivered in your voice
The output reads like you — not like an AI approximation of warmth. Because the material came from you, the speech has the specificity that signals authenticity. You'll recognise it as something you could actually stand up and deliver.

The Difference a Real Story Makes

Here's the same wedding toast, written two ways. The occasion: a best man speech. The groom is your university flatmate. He met his wife on a hiking trip he almost didn't go on because he was hungover from your birthday the night before.

They're a perfect match, and I know they're going to have a wonderful life together. Please join me in raising a glass to the happy couple.
✓ With NovaKit Skill
James almost didn't make it to that hiking trip. It was the morning after my birthday, and I found him at 7am eating toast in our kitchen looking like a man reconsidering every decision he'd ever made. He went anyway. He came back three days later and told me he'd met someone. I've never seen him look like that before, and I've known him for eleven years. Sarah — whatever you said up that mountain, thank you. He's been insufferably happy ever since, and I mean that as the highest possible compliment.

The first speech is polished and completely forgettable. The second one will be quoted at their anniversary dinner. The difference isn't craft — it's that the second speech was built from a specific memory, and that specificity is what makes it land. The Event Speech Writer skill is designed to pull that memory out and build around it.
