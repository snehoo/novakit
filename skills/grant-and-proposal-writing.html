Block A · Executive Summary
Standalone readable. Hooks a programme officer in 30 seconds.
Gates Foundation · Agricultural Innovation · 18-month grant period

In sub-Saharan Kenya, 2.3 million smallholder farmers lose an estimated 38% of harvests annually to late blight — a preventable disease with no affordable early-detection tool at the farm level. Kilimo Digital will deploy 240 AI-enabled crop sensors across Kisumu and Siaya counties, training 1,800 farmers to identify and treat blight within 48 hours of onset. By month 18, we project a 60% reduction in post-infection crop loss among participating farmers, translating to an average income recovery of $340 per household. This 18-month pilot requests $480,000 and is designed for handoff to county agricultural extension services by year three.
Problem → organisation → activities → measurable outcomes → ask → sustainability — in 130 words.
Block B · Needs Statement
Evidence-stacked. Local data first. Root cause named.
Late blight (Phytophthora infestans) is responsible for 38% of annual crop losses among smallholder potato and tomato farmers in Kisumu and Siaya counties — a combined population of 420,000 households living below the $2.15/day poverty line [Kenya National Bureau of Statistics, 2024]. Existing detection relies on visual inspection; by the time lesions are visible, the 48-hour treatment window has typically closed.

Extension officer ratios stand at 1:2,400 farmers, against the FAO-recommended 1:400 [FAO, 2023]. The gap is not awareness — 94% of surveyed farmers could name the disease — but timely, farm-level detection capability.

Why now: Sensor costs have fallen 67% since 2022 [GSMA AgriTech Index, 2025], making this model viable at sub-$300 per farm for the first time.
Root cause named. Three distinct data sources. Gap analysis in paragraph two. Urgency anchored to a real cost shift.
Block C · Logic Model
Inputs → Activities → Outputs → Outcomes → Impact. No implicit chains.
INPUTS → $480k grant · 3 agronomists · 240 sensors · county MOU
ACTIVITIES → Deploy sensors · Train farmers · Alert system · Monthly data review
OUTPUTS → 240 sensors live · 1,800 farmers trained · 4,320 alerts sent (est.)
OUTCOMES → 60% reduction in post-infection loss · $340 avg income recovery
IMPACT → Replicable county-level model · extension service handoff
Assumptions: mobile connectivity ≥60% · county partnership holds · farmer uptake ≥75%
If-then chain explicit. Assumptions listed. Risk response in the narrative below the table.
Block D · Budget Narrative
Every line justified. Maps directly to project activities.
Personnel — $198,000 (41%)
Lead agronomist: 1.0 FTE × $36k × 18 months. Manages deployment and farmer training cohorts.
Sensors & Hardware — $144,000 (30%)
240 units × $600 each. Unit cost verified against three supplier quotes; includes 12-month warranty and SIM provisioning.
Direct Programme — $86,400 (18%)
Training events: 60 sessions × $480 (venue, transport, materials). Directly tied to Activity 2 timeline.
Indirect / Overhead — $51,600 (11%)
Gates-permitted 10.75% indirect rate on modified total direct costs.
Every line maps to a project activity. No lump sums. Overhead rate explicitly cited against funder policy.
