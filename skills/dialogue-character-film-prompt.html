Shot 1 · Speaking
The dialogue shot. Lip-sync cue leads.
Close-up. Character lock: late 30s South Asian woman, long dark wavy hair, dark brown eyes, warm olive skin, worn denim jacket with silver pin on lapel.

She softly says, "I never stopped looking for you." Eyes hold steady on camera for a beat, jaw slightly tight. Slow dolly-in as she speaks. Warm practical window light from frame-left. Shallow depth of field, slight film grain.

Audio: near-silent room, distant low hum, no music — only her voice. Continuity: silver jacket pin visible, same focal length into next shot.
Emotion labelled before the line. Character description repeated verbatim. One motion verb. Audio spec included.
Shot 2 · Reaction
The listener's response. Separate prompt, separate shot.
Medium shot. Character lock: early 40s East Asian man, short grey-flecked black hair, sharp dark eyes, faded olive field jacket.

He stares for a beat. Eyes drop to the floor, then return slowly to camera. Jaw tightens. Corner of mouth pulls into a half-smile that doesn't reach his eyes. Static camera, handheld micro-drift only.

Warm amber practicals behind him. Same film grain as Shot 1. Continuity: olive jacket, same room, window light source held.
Reaction is its own prompt — not combined with the dialogue shot. Micro-expressions are directions, not descriptions.
Shot 3 · Coverage
Over-the-shoulder. Spatial relationship locked.
Over-the-shoulder. Her shoulder and partial profile foreground-left. His face in focus, background-right.

He quietly says, "I know." Shoulders drop slightly as he says it — the weight leaving, not arriving. Slow rack focus from his eyes to her shoulder as silence holds after the word.

Cooler fill light on his face vs warmer key on her jacket — colour temperature contrast holds through cut. Audio: room tone holds, no music enters yet. Continuity: silver pin still visible on her jacket shoulder.
OTS framing establishes spatial relationship between characters. Colour temperature contrast creates visual contrast without a cut.
