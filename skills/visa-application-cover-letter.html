Cover Letter · Schengen
Opening paragraph — purpose, identity, and travel dates stated immediately.
Re: Schengen Visa Application — Tourist — France (Primary Destination)

I, Ayesha Nair, a Sri Lankan national (Passport No. N1234567), respectfully apply for a Schengen tourist visa to visit France from 14 July to 28 July 2026 (15 nights). France accounts for 11 of my 15 nights; I will also spend 4 nights in Belgium. My application is submitted at the French consulate in accordance with Schengen rules.
Full name, nationality, passport number, visa type, exact dates, primary country of most nights — all in the first sentence as consulates require.
Ties to Home Country
The section that determines approval — ties stated with specifics.
Ties to Sri Lanka and Intent to Return

I am employed full-time as a Marketing Manager at Colombo Brands Pvt Ltd (since March 2021). My employer has confirmed my approved leave and guaranteed re-employment upon return (employment letter enclosed). I own a residential property in Colombo (title deed enclosed) and am the primary financial provider for my parents, who reside with me. My return flight departs Paris on 28 July 2026.
Specificity wins: named employer, property, family dependency, confirmed return flight. Each point maps directly to a supporting document.
Financial Position
Funds stated in exact figures, benchmarked against consulate thresholds.
Financial Capacity

I have sufficient funds to cover all travel expenses without working in the Schengen area. My bank statements for the past six months show a consistent balance averaging LKR 2,850,000 (approximately €8,200), well above the Schengen benchmark of €50–100 per day for the duration of my stay. Pre-booked accommodation (Hotel Marais, Paris — confirmation enclosed) accounts for the majority of my estimated expenses.
Six months of consistent balance — not a one-time deposit. Named hotel, confirmed booking. Figures converted to euros for the officer's reference.
