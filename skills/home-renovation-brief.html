Project Intent
Sets the vision before a single quote is requested.
Project: Master Bathroom Renovation — 8.5 m²

This renovation replaces a dated builder-grade bathroom with a calm, spa-adjacent space suited to daily use. Priority is tactile material quality over statement design. The space should feel considered and restful, not photographed.

Style reference: Warm Japanese minimalism — Hinoki timber, honed travertine, deep charcoal grouting, recessed niches. No floating vanities with concealed plumbing (maintenance risk).

Non-negotiables: Floor-to-ceiling tiling, heated floor, thermostatic shower valve, natural light preserved.
Project intent statement orients every trade before they open the specs. Sets expectations, not mood boards.
Material Specifications
Named products with dimensions. No vague callouts.
Floor: Travertine honed tile, 600×300mm, Ivory Vein Cross-Cut — Mandarin Stone or equivalent. Electric underfloor heating mat beneath.

Walls (wet zone): Porcelain in Charcoal Slate finish, 1200×600mm, large format. Grout: Mapei Ultracolor Plus FA, Anthracite.

Vanity: Bespoke solid oak, oiled finish — site-measured. No MDF carcass. Stone top: Honed Nero Marquina, 30mm thickness.

Shower valve: Thermostatic concealed — Hansgrohe ShowerSelect or Vado Notion equivalent.
Every callout includes manufacturer, finish, and dimension. Prevents "close enough" substitutions mid-build.
