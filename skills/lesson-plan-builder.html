Lesson Header · Grade 7 Science
Objective and materials. Clarity from the first line.
Topic: Photosynthesis — from sunlight to sugar
Grade: 7 · Duration: 50 minutes
Objective: Students will be able to explain how plants convert light energy into chemical energy, identifying the role of chlorophyll, CO₂, water, and sunlight.

Materials: Leaf specimens (1 per pair), whiteboard markers, printed diagram handout, coloured pencils, exit ticket slips (pre-cut).
Standards-aligned objective written in "students will be able to..." format. Materials named specifically — not "worksheets."
Hook · 5 minutes
Opens with curiosity. Captures attention before the phone does.
Hold up a leaf. Ask: "This leaf made its own lunch today. How?"

Give students 60 seconds to write their best guess on a sticky note. Take 3–4 responses aloud — don't correct yet. Post them on the board under "Before."

Prompt: "By the end of this lesson, you'll know exactly why some of these are right and some aren't."
Activates prior knowledge without revealing answers. Creates an unresolved loop the lesson closes. No tech required.
Differentiation · Support + Extension
Tiered for every learner. No one left behind or bored.
Support (struggling learners):
Provide a partially completed diagram with labels to match. Use sentence starters: "Chlorophyll absorbs… because…" Partner with a peer for guided practice.

Extension (advanced learners):
Challenge: "What happens to photosynthesis rate on a cloudy day? On a very hot day?" Students sketch a prediction graph and write a one-paragraph hypothesis.

EAL / ELL support: Bilingual glossary card for key vocabulary.
Every plan includes both support scaffolds and extension tasks. Single-track lessons are incomplete planning.
Formative Assessment · Exit Check
3-minute exit ticket. Know who's ready before the next lesson.
3-2-1 Exit Ticket (last 3 minutes):

Students write on a slip:
· 3 things photosynthesis needs
· 2 things it produces
· 1 question they still have

Collect at the door. Sort into three piles: "Got it," "Nearly," "Re-teach." Use pile 3 as the warm-up hook for the next lesson.
Quick formative check closes every plan. Tells you who's lost before the unit test — not after.
