Lunar New Year · Cultural Brief
Internal compass for CNY — what the brand needs to know first.
CULTURAL CONTEXT — Lunar New Year × Lifestyle Brand

Lunar New Year marks the start of a new cycle on the lunisolar calendar — celebrated as Chinese New Year (CNY), Vietnamese Tết, and Korean Seollal. The name matters: use "Lunar New Year" unless your audience is specifically Chinese.

This year's zodiac animal carries specific symbolism that shapes every visual and copy decision. Red and gold are expected; the distinction is in what you do beyond them.

Emotional register: renewal, family togetherness, and forward momentum — not spectacle.
Internal team brief. Never published. Sets the campaign compass before a single word of copy is written.
Eid al-Fitr · Message Strategy
Tone and copy direction — GCC audience, B2B brand.
CORE MESSAGE: After a month of reflection, Eid is the joy that follows patience.

WHAT THIS GREETING IS: A sincere, unhurried acknowledgement of the celebration — no promotion attached.

WHAT THIS GREETING IS NOT: An Eid sale. A crescent-moon graphic with the logo. One post for the entire GCC.

Lead with: The feeling of the occasion — not the brand name.
Language note: "Eid Mubarak" (Arabic: عيد مبارك) for broad audience; "Eid Sa'id" for Egyptian and Levantine markets. Native-speaker review required before publishing.
Separate messaging windows exist for Ramadan, Eid al-Fitr, and Eid al-Adha — this brief is Eid al-Fitr only.
