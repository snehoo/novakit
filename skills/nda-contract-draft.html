CLAUSE 3 — DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION [✓ STANDARD]

"Confidential Information" means any non-public technical, financial, commercial, or operational data disclosed by either party, whether in written, oral, or electronic form, and designated as confidential at the time of disclosure or within seven (7) days thereafter.

Plain English: Both sides agree to keep each other's business information private — but only information they've specifically labelled as confidential counts. Verbal disclosures must be confirmed in writing within one week.
Specific definitions improve enforceability. Vague "all information" clauses are routinely challenged and may void the entire agreement.

CLAUSE 7 — NON-COMPETE [⚠️ ATTORNEY REVIEW REQUIRED]

For a period of twelve (12) months following termination, Recipient shall not directly engage in any business activity substantially similar to Disclosing Party's core business within the Territory.

Attorney flag reason: Non-compete enforceability varies sharply by jurisdiction. This clause is unenforceable as written in California and restricted in the UK. Territory and duration must be reviewed against local law before execution.
Every high-risk clause carries a specific flag reason — not a generic "have a lawyer check this".

CLAUSE 9 — AI & MACHINE LEARNING RESTRICTION [✓ STANDARD — 2026]

Recipient shall not use, upload, or otherwise submit any Confidential Information to any artificial intelligence, machine learning, or large language model system — whether internal or third-party — without prior written consent from the Disclosing Party.

Plain English: The other party cannot feed your trade secrets into AI tools like ChatGPT or Claude. Omitting this clause is a critical oversight in any 2026 commercial NDA.
This clause is now a standard requirement. The skill flags its inclusion in every NDA produced.
