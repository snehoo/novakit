Slide 2 · Problem
Opens with the person in pain — not a macro trend.
Investor question: "Is this a real, large, painful problem?"

Operations managers at 50–200 person logistics firms spend 8–12 hours a week reconciling carrier invoices by hand. The errors they find are real — averaging 4.2% overcharge per billing cycle — but the dispute window closes in 72 hours. Most errors are caught after it closes.

At $2M in annual carrier spend, that's $84K leaking out every year with no flag, no alert, and no fix.
Named persona. Economic harm quantified. Urgency built without hyperbole. Anti-pattern avoided: no "the world is changing" opener.
Slide 3 · Why Now
Pins the unlock to a specific event, not vague timing.
Investor question: "Why is this the moment to solve it?"

Three things changed in 2024–2025 that make this solvable now and not in 2019: carrier EDI data became universally accessible via open APIs (previously locked to enterprise TMS vendors); LLM-based document parsing hit 97%+ accuracy on invoice formats; and post-ZIRP logistics operators are under margin pressure for the first time in a decade.

The window is open. The infrastructure exists. The buyer pain is acute.
Three specific unlocks with dates. Makes the investor feel the window is closing. Replaces vague "tailwinds" with named technology and economic shifts.
Slide 8 · Moat
Names the defensibility — not just "we use AI."
Investor question: "Why won't an incumbent kill this?"

The moat is a data flywheel, not a feature. Every invoice processed trains a dispute-pattern model specific to carrier routing profiles. Customers on the platform for 12 months have a 94% dispute capture rate vs. 61% at month one. The longer they stay, the better it gets — and the harder it is to leave.

Legacy TMS vendors hold the workflow. We hold the intelligence layer on top of it.
Specific flywheel named. Retention data used as moat evidence. Incumbent relationship reframed as a distribution advantage, not a threat.
Narrative Logic Note
The throughline that connects every slide.
Seed stage · VC/institutional · 12-slide standard

This deck makes one bet: that real-time invoice intelligence is the wedge into a full logistics ops platform. The sequence is traction-forward — the strongest signal appears on slide 5, before the market size, because at seed stage proof beats projection. The moat slide is weighted as the close argument, not a compliance checkbox.
Every output includes a narrative logic note — the throughline summary that tells you why the slides are in this order, for this stage and audience.
