Opening · Grounding the room
Introduces the speaker and the person being honoured.
My name is Sarah. Margaret was my mother. She was also, depending on the day, my biggest critic and my loudest champion — sometimes in the same phone call.

I want to tell you a few things about her. Not the summary version. The real version — the one that explains why this room is full.
Opening grounds the room before the first story. No dictionary definitions. Speaker introduces themselves and their relationship first.
Story · Specific and named
A single memory that shows who they were.
When I was eleven, I failed my maths exam. Not narrowly — I got seventeen percent. I walked home certain I was going to be in trouble.

Mum sat me down at the kitchen table, looked at the paper, and said: "Right. We're going to figure this out together." Not: "you should have studied." Not: "this is disappointing." She made tea, she got out a notebook, and we sat there for three hours until fractions made sense.

That was her. She didn't catastrophise. She just started working on the problem.
Specific story. Named detail (seventeen percent, kitchen table, fractions). Shows character — doesn't just state it.
Thematic thread · The close
Draws the stories together into one lasting idea.
I've been trying to find the word for what Mum did — in that kitchen, and in every version of that moment across forty years.

I think the word is steady. She was the steadiest person I've ever known. Not unshakeable — she had fears like anyone. But she didn't let her fears become yours.

If you're here today, she was steady for you too. In some way, at some point, she sat with you at your kitchen table.
Thematic thread named in a single word. Widened to include others in the room. Connects the personal to the shared.
