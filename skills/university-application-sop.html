Opening · The Hook

During my final-year thesis on federated learning privacy guarantees, I kept hitting the same wall: the theoretical bounds held, but the empirical attack surface on heterogeneous edge devices behaved nothing like the model predicted. That gap — between formal privacy proofs and real-world adversarial behaviour — is where I intend to plant my PhD research.

My application to Carnegie Mellon's CyberLab concentrates on exactly this fault line.

Why This Program · Paragraph 5
Named faculty + specific research connection — not generic praise.
Professor Lujo Bauer's work on usable privacy — specifically the adversarial evaluation framework in his 2024 IEEE S&P paper — connects directly to the attack modelling I could not complete in my undergraduate lab. CMU's CyLab also offers the only graduate seminar I've found that treats formal verification and empirical security as co-equal methodologies rather than competing schools of thought.

I would bring to this cohort a background straddling both.
Two program-specific references minimum: a named faculty member with a specific paper cited, and a course or lab only that program offers.
Research Experience · Paragraph 3
Specific project, method, finding — not a CV restatement.
At the IIT Bombay Systems Lab I spent fourteen months building and breaking a differential privacy pipeline for hospital readmission prediction. The finding that ended my thesis — that epsilon calibration under non-IID distributions degrades faster than Dwork's original bound suggests — was the result I least expected and the question I most want to keep pulling on.
Names the lab, the role, the method, and the specific finding. Connects backward to preparation and forward to the research gap the applicant wants to address.
